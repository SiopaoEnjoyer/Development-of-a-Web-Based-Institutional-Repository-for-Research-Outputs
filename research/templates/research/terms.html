2.1 Compliance with Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173)

The Repository operates in full compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012. Personal information collected and stored includes:

  • Name, email address, and birthdate for authentication purposes
  • Academic information (grade level, strand, batch year)
  • Authorship and research contribution data
  • Consent status for public display of full names

2.2 Data Collection and Usage

  • Minimal Collection: Only necessary personal data is collected
  • Purpose Limitation: Data is used solely for Repository functionality and institutional record-keeping
  • Access Control: Personally identifiable information (PII) is accessible only to authorized administrators and subject teachers
  • Data Retention: Personal data is retained only as long as necessary for educational and archival purposes

2.3 User Rights

Users have the right to:

  • Access their personal information stored in the system
  • Request correction of inaccurate personal data
  • Object to processing of personal data (subject to institutional requirements)
  • Request deletion of account (subject to archival and legal retention requirements)

2.4 Security Measures

  • Email verification for account authentication
  • Role-based access control (RBAC)
  • SSL/TLS encryption for data transmission
  • Regular security audits and updates

3.1 Compliance with IP Code of the Philippines (RA 8293)

All research outputs uploaded to the Repository are subject to the Intellectual Property Code of the Philippines. The Repository respects and protects the intellectual property rights of authors, BTCSI, and third parties.

3.2 Ownership of Research Outputs

  • Student Works: Research papers produced by students as part of academic requirements are considered collaborative works between students and BTCSI
  • School Property: BTCSI retains institutional rights to preserve, archive, and display approved research outputs
  • Author Attribution: Student authors retain moral rights and proper attribution

3.3 Copyright Notices and Attribution

  • Author names are clearly displayed (subject to consent status)
  • Research outputs may only be used for educational, non-commercial purposes
  • Proper citation is required when referencing Repository content

3.4 Permission and Consent Requirements

Before research outputs are publicly displayed in the Repository:

  • Student authors must provide explicit permission
  • For minor students (under 18), parental/guardian consent is required
  • Authors may control whether their full name or initials are displayed publicly
  • Consent may be withdrawn, subject to institutional archival policies

3.5 Prohibited Uses

Users may NOT:

  • Reproduce, distribute, or publicly display Repository content without permission
  • Use content for commercial purposes
  • Plagiarize or claim authorship of others' work
  • Remove or alter copyright notices, author attributions, or metadata

5.1 Account Access and Approval

  • Email verification is required for all logins

5.2 Browsing and Access Rights

  • Students may browse, search, and view research outputs in the Repository
  • Full-text PDF downloads require an approved account
  • Unapproved students may view metadata but cannot download files

5.3 Consent for Name Display

  • Students 18+: May provide consent directly to display full name
  • Students under 18: Must submit parental consent form (PDF) for approval
  • Without Consent: Names displayed as initials (e.g., "Dela Cruz, J. A.")

5.5 Research Submission

  • Students do not upload research directly; teachers upload on their behalf
  • Only research that has passed final defense is eligible for upload
  • Students may request removal of their research (subject to archival policies)

6.1 Account Privileges

When uploading research outputs, teachers must ensure:

  • PDF files are free from errors and properly formatted
  • Content complies with BTCSI academic integrity policies

6.3 Quality Control

  • Teachers are responsible for verifying the quality and originality of uploaded research
  • Uploads may be subject to administrative review before publication
  • Teachers may edit or remove research outputs if errors are discovered

6.4 Consent Management

  • Teachers may review parental consent forms submitted by students under 18
  • Consent approvals/denials must be processed within a reasonable timeframe
  • Denial of consent must be communicated with clear reasoning

6.5 Professional Conduct
